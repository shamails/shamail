"What's my fortune?"
I don't believe in fortune telling or fortune teller. However, I was raised by my very superstitious parents. (think "don't shake your keys, we'll get into a fight") So I took a deep dive into learning and researching different forms of fortune telling. This led to me asking several different types of fortune tellers to tell me my fortune. Through this book, I just wanted to express this experience without showing bias. I just wanted to share what exactly happened. The book is split into sections. The tan sections are all of the research and indepently figure information about different forms of fortune telling. The blue sections are based around actual readings that I got, sharing what they said and how it impacted me.
Gallery Gap
As someone who typically welcomes technological advances, the growth of AI/AI "art" made me stop and think about what is art? The discussion of what art is has been surround art for as long as art has been around. Through my own personal analysis I came to the decision that art needs to be human made. There are so many forms of art, all drawn us in and entrance us, because they are so viscerally human. However, I thought of a solution. The concept of a "virtual gallery for virtual art", a space for AI "art" to exist without taking the space of human made art. Everything inside the gallery was generated by a GAN, as well as the writing in the gallery book.
Jo
As someone who sincerely loves movies and especially likes period pieces, when Little Women came out I was hooked. Me being 19 when it came out, I watched the movie more times than I can count. Since the movie lived in my mind without forgeting, I did what any designer would do, make a book based on the screenplay. Every page in the book is every line containing the main character's name. The cover is the first line, and back cover being the last line.
Shamail's Type
I had, what felt like, a strange desire to type my notes in my own handwriting. To me, handwriting is such an idetifying feature and I felt like my notes didn't feel like my notes because they weren't in my handwriting. I went through the process of creating a typeface that is based around my handwriting, and I did have the feeling that my notes felt more like me
