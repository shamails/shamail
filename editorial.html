"What's my fortune?"
I don't believe in fortune telling or fortune tellers. However, I was raised by my very superstitious parents (think "don't shake your keys, we'll get into a fight" type of parents). So I took a deep dive into learning and researching different forms of fortune telling. This led to me asking several different types of fortune tellers to tell me my fortune. Through this book, I just wanted to express this experience without showing bias. I just wanted to share what exactly happened.

The book is split into sections. The beige sections are all of the research I have done and trying to figure out information about different forms of fortune telling.

The blue sections are based around actual readings that I got, sharing what they said and how it impacted me at the moment of the reading.
Jo
As someone who sincerely loves movies and especially likes period pieces, when Little Women (directed by Greta Gerwig) came out I was hooked. Me being 19 when it came out, I watched the movie more times than I can count. Since the movie lived in my mind for months and months, I did what any designer would do, make a book based on the screenplay.

Every page in the book is every line containing the main character's name, both spoken lines and stage directions. The cover being the first line, and back cover being the last line.
Shamail's Type
I had, what felt like, a strange desire to type my notes in my own handwriting. To me, handwriting is such an idetifying feature and I felt like my notes didn't feel like my notes because they weren't in my handwriting. This led me to go through the process of creating a typeface that is based around my handwriting, and I did suddenly have the feeling that my notes felt more like me. Due to this, I wanted to use my handwriting in one of my designs.

Specifically a redesigning of David Foster Wallace's "Consider the Lobster". I used my handwriting to stylize the footnotes to look the notes the reader would take in the margins, as the footnotes got longer, the more the "notes" would bleed into the main body text.
Gallery Gap
As someone who typically welcomes technological advances, the growth of AI/AI "art" made me stop and think about what is art? The discussion of what art is has been surround art for as long as art has been around. Through my own personal analysis I came to the decision that art needs to be human made. There are so many forms of art, all drawn us in and entrance us, because they are so viscerally human. However, I thought of a solution. The concept of a "virtual gallery for virtual art", a space for AI "art" to exist without taking the space of human made art. Everything inside the gallery was generated by a GAN, as well as the writing in the gallery book. Thought the content is computer generated, the project still requires a human touch. I was the one who designed the gallery, I was the one who designed the gallery book, I was the one who bound the book, even though all of the content was computer made.
Mean Type
Type specimens with unique presentations and themes make me incredibly interested in the type and excited to use it. Although I haven't made a typeface (at this point), I was inspired by a type I very much like, Mission Gothic, and I created these type specimens. The theme around them was based around gossiping and talking bad about other typefaces. Some using the background of the typeface as "insults" and others what we, generally, think of the typefaces. !I am not associated with Mission Gothic. I just like it a lot!
